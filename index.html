Motherboard

The next part is the motherboard, the brain of the computer. When picking one make sure that it is a mid-tower or a atx. You want to choose one with the matching socket type of your processor. Lastly, for ram you want to choose the speed of your PC. I suggest using a 12Gb ram for casual using or 32Gb for gaming. Just make sure it is a DDR4 motherboard support and DDR4 ram.

Storage

For storage, I suggest to get a SSD drive, which is more expensive, to install your operating system. You can either get another large SSD for more storage or get a HDD since they are bigger storage drives and cheaper.

Computer Case

Once all your parts are chosen you want to choose the right computer case. Most cheap cases tend to be heavier then somewhat expensive cases. You also want to make sure that all your chosen parts are able to fit. Lastly, you want to make sure that their is a lot of airflow for the computer to be kept cool.
